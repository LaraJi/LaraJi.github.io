Adversarial Reprogramming of Neural Networks

Posted on 2018-07-29 | Post modified: 2018-08-16 | In Paper | |
Words count in article: 354 word | Reading time ≈ 2 min

Arxiv Loc

Motivation and Contribution

Reprogramming the DNN to perform a new task $g(\tilde{x})$, different from the original task $f(x)$ :

  • the inputs of the two tasks, $x$ and $\tilde{x}$, can come from two different domains;
  • for simplification, this can be considered to learn two mapping functions (adversarial reprogramming functions): $h_f(.;\theta)$ and $h_g(.;\theta)$, where $h_f(\tilde{x};\theta)$ generates a valid input to function $f$, and $h_g$ maps outputs of $f(h_f(\tilde{x};\theta))$ back to outputs of $g(\tilde{x})$;
  • by adjusting $\theta$, s.t. $h_g(f(h_f(\tilde{x}))) = g(\tilde{x})$;
  • no perturbation magnitude limitation；
  • exploit non-linear property of the target model.

Comparison with Adversarial Samples

Adversarial Samples tries to generate targeted/untargeted inputs with a specific hardcoded output.

Comparison with Transfer Learning

Experiment

Original task: ImageNet classification

Adversarial task: counting squares, MNIST classification and CIFAR-1O classification

Observations:

  • Can achieve great reprogramming performance on counting and MNIST task, as well as moderate accuracy on CIFAR-10 classification;
  • On MNIST, adversarial program show architecture-specific property, Inception v.s. ResNet;
  • Some visual similarity of additive input among different tasks;
  • Adversarial training has little effect on defending from adversarial reprogramming;
  • Adversarial reprogramming can hardly achieve the similar performance on models with random weights(without training), and the generated adversarial programs are different between trained models and untrained ones.

Discussion

  1. A novel form of transfer learning, show the flexibility/reusability of DNN models. –> how to achieve better performance for more complicated tasks;
  2. Work can be extended to other domains, especilly for Turing-complete RNN models;
  3. Can be disastrous.

Questions or Inspirations
